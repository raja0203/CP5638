Aquapella

About Aquapella

Aquapella are 50 singers from the Townsville area bringing you a cappella world music at its very best
Songs given by this groups:-
  • A South African party song
  • a Hungarian love triangle
  • The Lion Sleeps Tonight in french
  • a Mexican lullaby

About Aquapella:-

Choir director Beat Lehmann was born in Switzerland where he first became involved in choir music by participating in classical productions like Handel's Messiah, Mozart's Mass in C minor etc.
After completion of his studies he concentrate on his music and dance career.In 1986 Beat migerated to Australia, completed his study and involved himself in choir music again. For 12 years he conducted AkaBella, a well-known world music choir from Bellingen, NSW.
Beat moved to Magnetic Island in 2003 and has established a similarly successful world music choir for the Townsville area. Beat's choirs focus on the rich musical heritage of cultures around the globe.

Poms from Oz

About Poms from Oz

Poms from Oz are Judy, Kathleen and Alan Pomeroy, a family of solo vocalists from North Queensland, Australia. All are dinky di Oz with Judy and Kathy being descendents of First Fleeters.
Their tight harmony and vocal strength bring a fresh feel to original, contemporary and traditional works, uncovering a rich tapestry of songs gathered in their travels from around the world. Most songs are a cappella but sometimes accompanied by guitar and/or harmonica. Moods in their songs range from downright to real tear jerkers and audience participation is encouraged. Their sound is distinctive, as Judy arranges all the songs specifically for their voices.

Stokes Nicholson Big Band

About Stokes Nicholson Big Band

The Stokes Nicholson Big Band was formed in 1991 through collaboration between the late Roy Stokes and Les Nicholson.
The band today demonstrates it has achieved the aims of both Roy and Les with experienced musicians arriving in town and feeling very proud to join such a powerful big band. A number of the original student members from 1991 are still in the band and have now joined the ranks of the experienced musicians and new students are still progressing from school bands to the Stokes Nicholson Big Band.
